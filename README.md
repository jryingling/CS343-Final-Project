# CS343-Final-Project

## Pokemon App

The app is based around providing a service for pokemon fans who want to track their favorite
pokemon / pokemon games, look at information about specific pokemon, and create /
experiment with custom teams.

### Functionality
- The foundational piece would be being able to access information about the pokemon
from the API https://pokeapi.co/. Here you would be able to access any information
about specific pokemon, and pokemon games.
- You would be able to track your favorite pokemon, your favorite games, and which
pokemon games you have played.
- An additional stat that could be tracked is the Pokedex. Each pokemon game has their
own pokedex, but it is harder to track all pokemon that exist. Through the website a user
could track all pokemon that they have caught between the games, to get an idea of the
total number of pokemon that they have.
- An important part of Pokemon is battling, and you are able to take a party of 6 into battle.
Pokemon is a strategy game so the composition of your team is important. The webapp
would allow you create custom teams, and experiment with swapping different pokemon
around to create a good team comp. The webapp wouldn’t be able to actually battle with
other people, as that is something that would require a game engine, but it would
provide something akin to a drawing board for people to create their own teams. You
would be able to customize the moveset, abilities, and items for each pokemon.

The expected user could be a casual fan to someone who takes it more seriously. A casual fan
may just be interested in reading some stats about their favorite pokemon, and keeping a list of
the ones that they like, and the games they have played. A more dedicated pokemon fan may
wish to keep track of the overall number of pokemon they have, and track them between
games. A serious pokemon fan may wish to experiment with different team compositions, so
that they can get creative and plan out a great pokemon team for battles.
